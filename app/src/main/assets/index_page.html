Introduction

The widget provides a quick view of many aspects of your Ford vehicle. Once you log in to your FordPass account through the app, enter your vehicle information, and place the widget somewhere on your phone's screen, it will periodically update to show your car's status.

The widget is shown below. Remember: You need to place the widget on your phone's screen

  1. long press on an unoccupied spot on your screen
  2. choose "Widgets"
  3. scroll down until you find this app
  4. press and drag the widget onto your screen

Using The App

All controls are located in the three-dot menu.

  • Select "Ford Log-in" to get started. Your user name and password are not stored.
  • Select "Manage Vehicles" to enter the VIN and nickname of your vehicle(s).
  • Select "Refresh" to force a query of Ford's servers.
  • Select "Choose Linked Apps" to choose which apps can be launched from the widget (located to the right of the Settings ("gear") icon
  • Select "Set Vehicle Color" if you want to modify the color of the vehicle displayed in the widget. You will also need to set "Enable color display of vehicles" under "Settings".
  • Select "Save Settings" or "Restore Settings" to back-up or restore your personal data (useful when the app must be removed prior to a new install)
  • Select "Settings" to change various application settings. You can control among other things:
    • set the frequency at which the widget updates
    • enable commands for lock/unlock and remote start when pressing the icons (future feature)
    • enable the location (if applicable) on the widget
    • display charging session information (see below)

Icon Information

Red is normally used to indicate something unusual happening when you're not using the car. The hood/frunk, trunk/hatch/tailgate, and doors will be highlighted in red to indicate they are not closed. Similarly, if a window is not fully closed, either a red icon will appear near the corresponding door (full-sized widget) or the actual window will appear in red (medium and miniature widgets).

The bell icon indicates the alarm status; green is armed, red is disarmed, and red with a "Z" indicates the vehicle is in deep sleep.

The ignition icon is green when the vehicle is on, gray when off, and yellow when remote start is active.

The information displayed for the 12V (low-voltage or LV) battery can show voltage, state of charge (SoC), or both. This is selectable under "Settings". When shown in a progress bar, green indicates the SOC is above 80%, yellow above 50%, and red below 50%.

Accessing Other Features

Tapping on various parts of the widget will give access to other features.

  • A single tap on the alarm icon will display the vehicle's interior temperature. This value is apparently only valid when the ignition is on.
  • A triple tap on the "Last refresh" text (or for the widget without "Last refresh", triple tap on the vehicle's image on the right) will show information about the last time the app updated with widget and the last time information was read from the servers.
  • The ignition icon controls remote start/stop and the lock icon controls lock/unlock (remote commands can be enabled under Settings). Double tap on the icon to send the command to the vehicle; it may take up to 30 seconds for the vehicle to respond. A yellow ignition icon indicates remote start is active.
  • The refresh icon on the lower left of the vehicle's image will send a command to the vehicle forcing it to refresh its status (this can be enabled under Settings). Warning: Since this has the potential to drain the 12V battery, the app limits how often you can perform a refresh in a 24 hour period.
  • Plug-in hybrids only: a single tap on the percentage progress bar will switch between gas and electric information.
  • Diesels only: a single tap on the low-voltage battery display will switch between LV battery, DEF level, and DEF range.
  • Electric amd plug-in only: a single tap on the plug icon will display the kilowatt-hours remaining in the high-voltage battery.
  • A single tap on all other locations will take you to this page.

Multiple Vehicles

If you own more than one vehicle compatible with FordPass, each vehicle's status can be accessed from the widget. To see the next vehicle:

  • Smaller widgets: double tap on the vehicle image on the right.
  • Large widget: single tap on your vehicle's image in the upper left corner.
You can use "Manage Vehicles" in the three-dot menu to choose which vehicle(s) the app will display.

Charging Information (for Plug-in Vehicles)

The charging status is indicated by icons for the charge plug and the high-voltage battery (HVB) . A red charge plug and gray battery icon indicate the car is unplugged; a green charge plug indicates the car is plugged in. When plugged in, the battery icon will indicate what is happening:

  • Not charging --
  • Charge scheduled --
  • Charge paused --
  • Charging --
  • Charge finished --

Information about AC charging sessions can be displayed on the widget by enabling "Display charge session info" in "Settings". When charging, the current power and total energy delivered will be shown on the charging progress bar. Information about DC fast charging can be display by also enabling "Display DCFC info". Once DC fast charging is detected by the app, it will automatically request updates from the servers every 30 seconds (note that you mey want to manually refresh once you initiate charging to have updates start sooner).

Enabling "Save DCFC logs" in addition to the above settings will allow you to view recent DC fast charging sessions via "View DCFC Logs" in the three-dot menu. If you are currently charging, the data is updated in real-time as information is read from the servers. The logs will show power, energy, and SOC during each charging session. Data for each session is stored for 180 days then deleted. All session data is deleted if this setting is disabled.

Per-app Language Preferences

If your phone is running Android 13 or later, you can select the language for the app. Under your phone's settings, find the app and click on "Language".
